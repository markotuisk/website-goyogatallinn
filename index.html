About Ashtanga

An Ancient Practice for Modern Life

Ashtanga yoga is a system of yoga recorded by the sage Vamana Rishi in the Yoga Korunta, an ancient manuscript.

The Eight Limbs

Ashtanga means "eight limbs" in Sanskrit, referring to the eight aspects of yoga described by Patanjali in the Yoga Sutras. These eight limbs are a progressive system that purifies the body and mind, leading to self-realization.

The practice combines flowing movements with breath (vinyasa), specific gazing points (drishti), and energy locks (bandhas) to create a moving meditation that builds strength, flexibility, and inner peace.

Our Space

A Sanctuary in the City

Located at Narva mnt 7D, Tallinn, our studio is a peaceful retreat on the 3rd floor, away from the city's hustle and bustle. Please note that access is by stairs only as there is no elevator in the building.

Designed with natural wood floors and calming earth tones, the space is filled with light and positive energy. We provide all the amenities you need for a comfortable practice, including high-quality mats, props, changing rooms, and a cozy tea corner for you to relax in before or after your class.
